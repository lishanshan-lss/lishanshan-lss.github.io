About me

I study memory, behavior, and the signals in between

I am a fourth-year PhD candidate in cognitive neuroscience. I study how the brain holds information in mind to support flexible behavior, using neuroimaging and computational modeling to link neural signals, memory representations, and behavior.

Project academic

Academic projects

I am part of a global PhD program and work across two labs: the Curtis Lab in New York City and the Sreenivasan Lab in Abu Dhabi.

How do we cope with effortful tasks?

In this study, we asked how effort sculps the memory we hold in mind. Using fMRI, we found that higher cognitive effort increased prefrontal activity, which in turns improved memory fidelity, linking effort, top-down control, and the quality of remembered information. I contributed the last part of analysis to this collaborative project.

Master, S. L., Li, S., & Curtis, C. E. (2024). Trying Harder: How Cognitive Effort Sculpts Neural Representations during Working Memory . The Journal of Neuroscience, 44(28), e0060242024.
